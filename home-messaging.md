# home-messaging.md — HOME page spec (exec / outcomes)

> HOME sells the **outcome** to the exec who signs. PRODUCT explains the
> **mechanism** to the evaluator. Same substance, higher altitude on HOME.
> The Channel Graph is the **hero spectacle** here (named in the subhead, shown
> assembling) — it is NOT re-explained step-by-step; that lives on PRODUCT.
>
> This supersedes the earlier 5-step HOME spine. HOME = hero + 4 outcome
> sections. Voice: short flat declaratives, concrete nouns, present tense,
> assert the outcome. Headlines ≤6 words, subheads ≤14. Anti-vacuous: every
> outcome ties to a number, a decision, or a meeting an exec owns.
> (10 options written + wince-tested per slot; 3 survivors shown.)

## Section 1 — Bring in more partner revenue  ·  graph: attribution paths (state 4)
**Diagnosis of the gap it fills:** the partner number is currently unprovable in the QBR.
**Headline — survivors:**
1. **Prove your partner number.** — names the board number; assert; 4 words.
2. **Partner revenue you can defend.** — ties to the QBR/board.
3. **Walk into the QBR with proof.** — names the meeting an exec owns.
**Body:** See sourced and influenced revenue, with the records — the number you take to the board.
**Bullets:**
- Sourced and influenced, with the records
- A number that holds up in the QBR
- Grow what you can finally measure

## Section 2 — Build your ecosystem  ·  graph: Channel TAM / ghosts + signals (state 3)
**Gap:** you're recruiting on gut, and consolidation blindsides you.
**Headline — survivors:**
1. **Recruit the partners you're missing.** — names the decision (recruit); 5 words.
2. **See consolidation before it hits.** — names the risk an exec owns; 5 words.
3. **See the gaps before competitors do.** — competitive framing; 6 words.
**Body:** See who fits your channel, who you're missing, and which moves to make before the market does.
**Bullets:**
- The right partners, not more partners
- Recruit the gaps by vertical and geography
- See M&A and consolidation coming

## Section 3 — Take full control of your program  ·  graph: tier clustering (state 5)
**Gap:** tiers/quota/investment are gut calls you can't defend. (NOTE: "take
control" is banned in the copy itself — name the real levers instead.)
**Headline — survivors:**
1. **Model the change before you commit.** — the decision an exec actually makes; 6 words.
2. **Set tiers and quota on evidence.** — names the real levers; 6 words.
3. **Defend every investment call.** — names the decision/meeting; 4 words.
**Body:** Set tiers, territories, quota, and investment on what's actually happening — and test a change before you make it.
**Bullets:**
- Tiers, territories, quota, investment
- Grounded in evidence, not gut
- Model a change before you commit

## Section 4 — Every question at your fingertips  ·  graph: query path (state 6)
**Gap:** every channel question is a two-week ops project.
**Headline — survivors:**
1. **Answers without the ops ticket.** — names the ops queue; 4 words. (Diverges from PRODUCT's "Ask your channel anything.")
2. **Skip the two-week ops project.** — names the pain; 5 words.
3. **Ask your channel anything.** — flattest; but collides with PRODUCT — avoid on HOME.
**Body:** Ask in plain language, get the answer with the records — no two-week ops project.
**Bullets:**
- Plain-language answers, records attached
- No ops ticket, no waiting
- For your team and your partners
