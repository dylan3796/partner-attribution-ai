# Covant

> The partner intelligence layer for B2B companies that sell through a channel.

## As a company

Covant is the **partner intelligence layer** for B2B companies that sell through a channel — resellers, agencies, ISVs, SIs. Their CRM runs the direct deals. Their PRM is where partner paperwork goes to die. And the actual partner revenue motion — who's working which deal, what a partner needs next, why anyone got paid — lives in spreadsheets, Slack threads, and the heads of a few overworked partner managers.

Covant is the system that motion runs on. Deals stay in the CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, Monaco) and partners register them *through* Covant. Everything on the partner side — credit calculated and explained, incentives and tier progress, payouts, and a next-best-action for every partner — lives in Covant, shared with both the vendor's team and the partners themselves through a portal they actually log into.

The wedge is **explainable attribution**: every dollar of partner credit ships with a *why*, so finance and partners both trust what pays out. That trust is how Covant earns the right to run the rest of the motion. The pitch is blunt: **keep your CRM, cut your PRM.**

## As a vision

The bet underneath the product: **partners are the most underused, most honest data in B2B software, and no tool has ever turned it into action.** Every partner interaction is a signal about where the business should grow next — and almost everyone throws it away by treating partners as a cost center.

Covant's vision is to be the place that captures that signal and acts on it. Not a dashboard you check quarterly — a loop:

```
capture the context legacy systems never had
  → reason over it the way a great partner manager would
    → act on it
      → write it back into the CRM
        → learn from the outcome
```

Legacy tools can only *report* on data someone half-logged. Covant *creates* the record by capturing what was never written down.

And the long game is a network, not just a tool. Partners work with many vendors at once. As they live in Covant's portal, Covant becomes the partner's **cockpit across all their vendor relationships** — and the data stays current because the partner's own payouts depend on it. At that point a vendor can't leave: their partners already work there.

## How it gets used — concrete scenarios

### 1. The deal a partner could have saved (PSM agent)
A $400K deal is stalling. The vendor's CRM shows nothing useful. Covant's PSM agent — which can read deals, partners, touchpoints, and contact data — spots that one of the company's resellers has a deep relationship with the buyer's IT director. It drafts a warm intro for the partner, surfaces it to the human as a proposal, and on approval logs the touchpoint and updates the CRM. The deal gets a co-sell motion it would never have gotten.
**Value:** revenue that was leaking out the bottom of the funnel, recovered.

### 2. The 40 contacts the CRM never had
A partner manager has spent a year in meetings with a key reseller and knows fifteen people there — none of it in the CRM. Covant captures that context from comms and meetings, maps the contact web at the partner, and writes it back into Salesforce, bidirectionally. When that partner manager leaves, the relationship doesn't walk out the door with them.
**Value:** institutional memory that used to evaporate, now an asset.

### 3. Payout that finance doesn't fight
End of quarter, a partner claims credit on a deal three other partners also touched. Instead of a black-box number finance discounts, Covant shows the touchpoint timeline, applies the org's chosen attribution model, and explains the split. The partner sees their credit in the portal in real time and trusts it; finance signs off without a meeting.
**Value:** faster payouts, fewer disputes, a partner-revenue line the CFO will actually stand behind.

### 4. Program decisions on evidence, not the loudest voice in the QBR
The team is deciding where to put next year's MDF and which partners to promote a tier. Covant's program intelligence shows, from the graph, which partners are quietly driving sourced pipeline and which are coasting on past glory. The investment goes where the evidence points.
**Value:** channel dollars allocated by what's working, not by gut.

### 5. Churn caught before renewal
A partner that used to register two deals a month has gone quiet — but nobody noticed because nobody was watching the signal. Covant's partner-health view flags the drop weeks before it would have surfaced at renewal, and recommends the re-engagement play.
**Value:** a partner relationship saved instead of post-mortemed.

### 6. The partner's side (the network unlock)
A reseller logs into Covant to see their pipeline, commissions, payouts, certifications, and MDF — across the vendors that run on Covant. They keep their deal status current because that's how they get paid accurately. The next vendor that wants to onboard them finds the partner already there, data already flowing.
**Value:** partners get a cockpit they'd refuse to give up; vendors get partners who pull other vendors in.

## Where the value lands

- **For the vendor's revenue team:** partner revenue becomes their most *provable* line instead of their most hand-wavy one — and deals get co-sell help that used to depend on one person remembering the right intro.
- **For finance:** auditable, explainable credit; payouts that don't trigger arguments.
- **For the partner-program leader:** investment, incentives, and tiers decided from the graph's evidence, with churn flagged early.
- **For the partners themselves:** a single, trustworthy view of their impact and money — the reason they keep the data fresh and the reason they'd resist any rip-out.

---

**The one sentence:** Covant turns the partner data every B2B company is sitting on into provable revenue, a program you can steer, and — over time — a channel network that compounds with every partner who uses it.
