# Covant — Vision & Value

How Covant brings revenue in the door and makes the partner program easier to run.
Drawn from the live site (Home, Company, Product), with the revenue and
partner-recommendation story pulled to the front.

---

## The Vision

**Covant's whole company hangs off one conviction:**

> **"Partners are signal, not overhead."**

The bet underneath it: **partners are the most underused data in B2B software** —
billions of datapoints about what's working and where to grow — and *no tool has
ever turned that data into action.* Most of the real channel work happens in the
partner's world (spreadsheets, emails, Slack, deal notes, marketplace portals)
and never crosses back into yours, so it stays invisible and unrewarded.

Covant fixes that with a new primitive — the **Channel Graph**: *partner data,
organized into one place both sides can act on.* Covant isn't "another PRM" or an
attribution tool; it's **the Partner Intelligence Layer** that sits on top of that
graph, proves the revenue partners actually drive, and — the part that matters for
growth — **tells you what to do next.**

Three beliefs frame it: partners are signal, not overhead; the relationship was
never the problem, the *blindness* was; and software should do the figuring-out,
so "better together" takes weeks instead of a decade.

---

## How it brings revenue in the door

This is where Covant stops being a reporting tool and becomes a growth engine.
Three compounding ways:

**1. We get past the attribution headache — and that headache was quietly costing
deals.** Every dollar gets credited *with the reason attached* — sourced or
influenced, one number you and your partners read the same way. No black-box
credit, no quarter-end attribution fights, no partner going cold because they
didn't trust they'd get paid. When partners trust the number, **they bring you
more deals.** Attribution isn't the product — it's the trust we earn before the
rest works.

**2. It's easier for partners to lock deals — and to see where they stand.** A
partner registers a deal in two minutes in your portal, it flows straight into
your CRM, every touch gets logged, and the incentive flags as earned the moment
it's earned (with your sign-off — Covant never moves the money). Less friction
means a higher close rate and a partner who comes back.

And independent of any tier structure: **every partner can see where they
stand** — their attributed revenue, their progress against whatever your program
measures, what they've earned. Some programs have a Gold tier and upward
mobility; many don't. Either way, visibility is what keeps a partner engaged —
knowing the number, knowing it's fair, knowing it's theirs. *Where there is* a
ladder, Covant shows the bar and the next move to climb it; *where there isn't,*
it still gives the partner an honest, current picture of their standing.

**3. The real unlock: Covant recommends partners.** Once the graph knows your
channel, it doesn't just record what happened — it tells you what to do:

- **When a deal stalls out, Covant surfaces the partner who can un-stick it** —
  the SI who's closed in that vertical, the co-sell partner already inside that
  account. Instead of watching pipeline rot, you get a recommendation to pull a
  partner in and save the deal.
- **When you're hunting for a partner — for an open deal, a new territory, a
  motion you can't cover — you just ask Covant.** The right partner is at your
  fingertips, ranked from your own channel-graph patterns, instead of buried in
  someone's memory or a spreadsheet.

That's the shift: from *"prove what partners did"* to *"put the right partner on
the right deal at the right moment, on demand."* Proving the number gets us in the
door; recommending the next move is what actually grows the number.

## How it makes things easier for customers

- **Keep your stack.** Covant rides alongside your CRM — the CRM stays system of
  record, registrations flow in, closed-deal data flows back to keep the graph
  current. Self-serve in ~15 minutes, not a six-month rollout.
- **Built for the missing middle** — Heads of Partnerships running multiple
  motions, partner managers buried in reconciliation, and CROs who just want a
  partner-revenue number they can trust.
- **Less manual grind, more selling.** The reconciliation, the credit arguments,
  the "who do we put on this deal?" scramble — Covant absorbs all of it.

## Examples of how

**The revenue loop** — *A partner brings you a deal:* they register it (two
minutes, your fields) → you approve and it flows into your CRM with every touch
logged → it closes → attribution splits with the reasons attached → the incentive
flags as earned, evidence included, awaiting your sign-off.

**The standing loop** — *A partner wants to know where they stand:* their portal
shows their attributed revenue, what they've earned, and their progress against
whatever your program tracks — current in real time, no chasing you for it. If
your program has tiers and mobility, it also shows the bar and the move that
closes the gap; if it doesn't, it's still the honest, always-on scoreboard
partners actually want.

**The stalled-deal save** — *Pipeline's going cold:* Covant flags the stall and
recommends the partner most likely to revive it — already inside the account or
proven in that vertical — so you act before the deal dies.

**The "find me a partner" ask** — *You've got an open deal or a new territory and
no obvious coverage:* ask Covant, and it hands you the ranked partner that fits,
drawn from your real channel data — no meeting, no spreadsheet hunt.

**The planning loop** — *Shaping next quarter:* partner scores and health stay
current, Covant ranks who to back and which partner belongs on which open deal,
forecasts partner-sourced revenue across programs — so you walk into the QBR with
the answers already pulled.
