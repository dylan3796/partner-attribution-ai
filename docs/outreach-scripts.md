# Covant — Outreach Playbook (Design-Partner Hunt)

*For the first 15-25 design-partner conversations before and around YC. Goal: find 5-8 companies willing to pilot Covant, give real feedback, and — if it works — become early paid customers with case-study rights.*

**Important:** this is a Year-1 wedge document. It intentionally ignores adjacent verticals (insurance, marketplaces, services) covered in `docs/vision.md`. Stay in B2B SaaS with partner programs.

---

## The ICP (who to actually reach out to)

Three filters, all must be true:

1. **B2B SaaS, Seed → Series B, ~$3M-$50M ARR.** Pre-$3M they don't have a partner program yet. Post-$50M they have entrenched tools (PartnerStack / Impartner) and an 18-month sales cycle.
2. **Has a Head of Partnerships (or equivalent) hired in the last 18 months.** That person is (a) the buyer, (b) operating under a mandate from the CEO/CRO to "show partner ROI this year," and (c) underwater. New hires = short time-to-decision and fewer incumbent sunk costs.
3. **Partner program is "active but chaotic"** — visible on LinkedIn / website (partner page exists, a partner tier system, some co-sell messaging) but no evidence of a mature PRM (no "Powered by PartnerStack" portal link, no Crossbeam integration callout).

### Signal sources (in priority order)

- **LinkedIn job-title search:** "Head of Partnerships," "Director of Partnerships," "VP Partnerships," "Partner Programs Manager," "Partner Operations." Filter: company size 50-500, joined role in last 18 months.
- **Pavilion / Partnership Leaders / Nearbound communities.** Members of these exact communities self-identify as the buyer.
- **Newly-announced partnerships in TechCrunch / SaaS press.** "Company X launches partner program" = they just hired the persona and haven't chosen tooling.
- **LinkedIn posts about partner attribution pain.** Anyone posting "why is partner attribution so hard in 2026" is a 95% close-rate inbound.
- **Cloud marketplace co-sell announcements (AWS / Azure / GCP).** Slightly ahead of wedge but qualifying — the same persona handles both.

### Named-account lists to build (~100 accounts each)

Build three lists in a tracker (Notion / Airtable / sheet):

1. **"New Head of Partnerships" list.** LinkedIn sales-nav saved search, new-title-in-role flag. Refresh weekly.
2. **"Partner page exists, no PRM signal" list.** Manual — 30 min/week scanning target-company /partners pages.
3. **"Pavilion partnerships-track members" list.** If you're in Pavilion, export the directory. Otherwise, build manually from public member lists.

Target: 300 named accounts by end of month one. Out of 300, ~60 will respond, ~20 will take a call, ~5-8 will pilot.

### Ranked target personas

1. **Head of Partnerships (primary).** Budget owner. Needs: show ROI fast, stop the quarterly attribution argument, report clean numbers to the CEO.
2. **Partner Sales Manager.** User. Needs: more qualified co-sell opportunities, less time chasing partners, faster intros.
3. **Partner Operations / RevOps for partnerships.** User. Needs: clean payouts, no disputes, audit trail.
4. **Head of RevOps / CRO** (secondary buyer at companies without a dedicated Partnerships head). Needs: attribution they can defend in a board meeting.

**Avoid for now:** CEOs (too high), Marketing leads (wrong budget), affiliate-only programs (wrong wedge — PartnerStack wins that), agencies/resellers (they're the partner, not the buyer).

## The outreach motion

Three channels, used in combination per account:

### Channel 1 — LinkedIn personalized DM (highest reply rate)

- **Connect first**, with a one-line note referencing something specific (a post, a shared connection, a new-role congrats).
- **Wait for accept** (typically 1-4 days).
- **Send the first message** within 24 hours of accept. See scripts below.
- **Follow up once**, 5-7 days later, with a second-angle message. Do not follow up a third time — move on.

### Channel 2 — Cold email (scaled backup)

- Use only after finding a verified work email (Apollo, Hunter, Clearbit, or website /about pages).
- Keep the email shorter than the LinkedIn DM — the bar is higher.
- Follow-up cadence: day 3, day 10. No third send.

### Channel 3 — Community presence (compounds over time)

- Post 1-2x/week in Pavilion / Partnership Leaders / Nearbound Slack about real, specific partner-attribution problems and solutions. Not pitching — teaching.
- Comment thoughtfully on LinkedIn posts from ICP personas. The goal is to become a recognized name *before* the outreach hits.

## Message templates

### Template A — LinkedIn DM, "New Head of Partnerships" angle

Use when the recipient started the role in the last 6 months.

> Hey {first_name} — congrats on the {company} role. Saw {they/they haven't} shipped a partner page yet and was curious how you're thinking about attribution and payout tooling in the first 90 days. I'm building Covant — the short version is "partner team runs on it, four role-specific agents do the work of people you haven't hired yet." Happy to send a 3-minute Loom walkthrough if useful, no pitch. No worries either way.

**Why this works:** congrats is honest (new role), the implicit question ("how are you thinking about attribution and payout tooling") makes them think rather than respond yes/no, the one-liner is memorable, the Loom ask is lower-friction than a call.

### Template B — LinkedIn DM, "I noticed your post" angle

Use when they've posted about partner attribution, co-sell, or program structure in the last 30 days.

> {first_name} — your post on {specific topic} hit a nerve. I've been building Covant to solve exactly that: a platform your partner team runs on, with four agents (partner sales, partner account, program, ops) that do the work of a team most companies haven't hired yet. Would you be up for a 20-minute call this or next week? Happy to share what we've built even if you're not in the market — getting real feedback from operators is the whole point right now.

**Why this works:** specific reference (proves it's not a template), frames the call as *getting feedback from them* (flattering + lower pressure), no salesy CTA.

### Template C — LinkedIn DM, Pavilion / community warm intro

Use when you share a community membership.

> Hey {first_name} — fellow {community} member here. I'm building Covant, an AI-native platform for partner teams — four persona agents (PSM, PAM, Program, Ops) built into the product so a 2-person partner team can run what used to take 6. Would love 20 minutes of your brain in exchange for a sneak peek and zero sales pressure. If the timing's off, totally fine — happy to just send a Loom.

### Template D — Cold email (no prior connection)

**Subject:** `{first_name}, quick question about {company} partner program`

> Hi {first_name},
>
> Short version: I'm the founder of Covant, an AI-native platform for partner programs. We ship it with four persona agents — partner sales, partner account, program, and ops — so teams that haven't fully hired a partner org can still run one end-to-end.
>
> Noticed {specific observation about {company} partner program — a tier on the /partners page, a recent partnership launch, a podcast mention}. Curious how you're handling attribution and payouts today.
>
> Worth 20 minutes to compare notes? If not, no worries — happy to send a 3-minute walkthrough video instead.
>
> — {your_name}
> {covant_url}

### Template E — Follow-up (any channel)

Send 5-7 days after the first message if no reply.

> Hey {first_name} — pulling this up. If partner attribution / payout tooling isn't a priority in the next 90 days, totally understood, just let me know and I'll stop bugging you. If it is, even a "yes but later" reply helps me prioritize who to build for.

**Why this works:** gives them an easy out, which paradoxically raises reply rates. The ask is "reply anything," not "book a meeting."

## Demo-call structure (when they say yes)

20 minutes, four beats:

1. **Discovery (5 min).** "Walk me through how you handle partner attribution and payouts today." Listen for: spreadsheets, quarterly arguments, manual Stripe transfers, no portal. Those are your pains.
2. **Tailored demo (10 min).** Show only the feature that solves what they just described. If they complained about payout ops, show the Ops Agent + Tier-0 onboarding. If they complained about finding co-sell, show PSM Agent + account mapping. Do not show everything.
3. **Agent moment (2 min).** "Here's what changes: your partner team is 1-3 people, and you're hiring slowly. Covant ships four agents that cover the roles you haven't filled yet. PSM Agent drafts the intro you haven't had time to write. Ops Agent catches the payout error before it goes out." Let that land.
4. **Design-partner ask (3 min).** "I'm looking for 5-8 design partners for a 60-day pilot. Free during pilot, locked-in pricing after. In exchange I need (a) weekly 30-min feedback, (b) permission to name you in the YC app if it helps, (c) a case-study conversation if the pilot works. Interested?"

**If yes:** send pilot agreement + kickoff link same day.
**If "maybe":** offer a 3-minute Loom walking through their specific use case by end of week.
**If "no":** ask what would have to be true for them to say yes in 6 months; log it.

## What to send right after the call (same day)

- Personalized recap email (3-5 sentences max).
- One specific article or teardown relevant to their program (not a generic whitepaper).
- Pilot agreement (if they said yes or maybe).
- Calendar invite for the next check-in (if piloting) or a 30-day "any update?" nudge (if maybe).

## Metrics to track per week

| Metric | Target (first 4 weeks) |
| --- | --- |
| Net-new named accounts added | 75/week |
| Personalized LinkedIn DMs sent | 40/week |
| Cold emails sent (with personal signal) | 25/week |
| LinkedIn connection accept rate | >40% |
| DM reply rate | >20% |
| Demo calls booked | 3-5/week |
| Design partners signed | 1-2/week (after week 2) |

If LinkedIn accept rate is <30%, your connect note is too templated. If DM reply rate is <10%, the opening line isn't personalized enough.

## Do-not-do list

- Do not pitch "AI" without immediately following with a concrete outcome. "AI-native partner platform" alone gets ignored. "Four agents that do the work your partner team hasn't hired yet" gets replies.
- Do not send a calendar link in the first message. Ask for a reply first.
- Do not mention YC unless they ask. It helps in the room, not in the cold message.
- Do not offer enterprise features, SSO, SOC 2 promises, or custom builds in the first call. You're finding design partners, not selling six-figure contracts.
- Do not chase after two follow-ups. Move on — LinkedIn and email are volume games plus compounding reputation.
