# Copy rewrite — voice pass (higher register)

> Bar: Monaco / Linear / Vercel / Ramp. Short, flat, confident declaratives.
> Concrete nouns, present tense. Assert the outcome; don't explain the mechanism
> or narrate a process. Headlines ≤6 words, subheads ≤14. Reference cadence:
> "Monaco does the updating, you do the selling."
>
> Process per slot: 10 options written and wince-tested ("would a senior brand
> marketer wince?"), cut to the 3 ranked survivors shown here. No code.

---

## REVISION — drop the bare word "graph"

"Channel Graph" is the product name (proper noun, used with context). The bare
word **"graph" is banned in copy** — it's jargon nobody parses. The thing it does
in plain terms: takes scattered partner data and **organizes it into one place** —
clear areas (Partners, Accounts, Opportunities, Program, Definitions, Personnel),
all connected. Sell **organized / one place / connected / your channel**, not
"graph." The neural-net visual already shows those areas as nodes.

### Hero — replacing "The first Partner Intelligence Engine for startups."
1. **Your whole channel, in one place.** — concrete, asserts the end state, nods to the organized-areas idea, zero jargon.
2. **Partner intelligence for startups.** — keeps your category; flat (cf. "the AI-native CRM"); no "graph."
3. **Every partner and deal, connected.** — concrete nouns; "connected" carries the structure without "graph."

### Lines from below, revised to drop "graph":
- Subhead: **All your partner data, organized in one place.** / **Covant maps your whole channel from data you already have.**
- S1: **Every system, one place.**
- S2: **Covant builds the first pass.** (or proper-noun once: "Covant builds your Channel Graph.")
- S6: **Ask your channel anything.**
- CTA heading: **See it on your data.** (replaces "See your Channel Graph.")

The original survivor lists below are kept for reference; where they use bare
"graph," prefer the revised lines above.

### Hero — current: "The first Partner Intelligence Engine for startups."
**Diagnosis: WORDY + ABSTRACT.** "The first" is a hype/claim flex; "Intelligence
Engine" stacks two abstractions; it names a category instead of asserting an
outcome. A brand marketer winces at "the first … Engine."
1. **Run your channel on one graph.** — concrete verb + noun, present tense, asserts the outcome, zero hype.
2. **Partner intelligence for startups.** — flat category line (cf. "the AI-native CRM"); drops the claim and the "Engine."
3. **The Channel Graph for startups.** — leads with your proprietary noun; flat and ownable.

### Subhead — current: "The AI-native platform that turns scattered partner data into one connected source of truth."
**Diagnosis: CORNY + OVERWRITTEN.** "the platform that turns X into Y" = banned
construction; "source of truth" = buzz-abstraction; explains mechanism; throat-clears.
1. **Covant maps your whole channel from data you already have.** — 9 words, concrete, present tense.
2. **Your partners, deals, and accounts in one graph.** — concrete nouns, flat.
3. **See who drives your channel, and what to do next.** — outcome-led.

### Section 1 — current: "Every source you already have feeds one graph."
**Diagnosis: WORDY.** "Every source you already have" pads; "feeds one graph" leans mechanism. 8 words.
